## TIER 1: PURSUE — Go After These

### 1. IRS Section 48E Elective Pay (Direct Pay) — Battery Storage Only
- **Score**: 22 | **Type**: Tax credit (direct cash payment) | **Source**: U.S. Treasury
- **Status**: ACTIVE — Elective Pay mechanism survived the One Big Beautiful Bill Act
- **Value**: 30% of battery storage project cost returned as cash. Bonus adders possible (domestic content +10%, energy community +10%).
- **Key Details**:
  - As a 501(c)(3), ECS qualifies for Elective Pay (IRS sends cash instead of requiring tax liability)
  - **Battery storage has full credits through 2033** — no time pressure
  - Solar credits are being phased out (BOC before July 4, 2026 required) — **too late for ECS to start solar under 48E**
  - Domestic content compliance is now MANDATORY for direct pay projects over 1 MW (100% haircut for non-compliance)
  - **Pre-filing registration MUST happen before equipment is placed in service**
- **RECOMMENDATION FOR VERONICA**: Position 48E exclusively for battery storage. Solar window is effectively closed for a school that hasn't started planning. Battery is solid through 2033.
- **Estimated Value**: On a $300K battery system → $90K-$150K cash back from IRS

### 6. Section 179D Energy Efficient Commercial Buildings Deduction — EXPIRING
- **Score**: 17 | **Type**: Tax deduction (up to $5.94/sq ft) | **Source**: U.S. Treasury
- **Status**: **BEING REPEALED** — OBBBA permanently eliminates 179D. Construction must begin before **June 30, 2026** to qualify.
- **Key Details**:
  - ECS cannot claim this directly (tax-exempt entity)
  - The law allows ECS to **allocate the deduction to their designer/contractor** — HVAC engineer, architect, or contractor
  - Designer claims up to **$5.94/sq ft** (2026 indexed rate) on their own tax return
  - ECS's leverage: "We'll allocate you this deduction if you reduce our project cost by $X"
  - Requires 25%+ energy savings vs ASHRAE 90.1, energy modeling, and third-party certification
  - **ECS is already mid-HVAC project** → construction has begun → they're before the June 30 cutoff (GOOD)
- **URGENT ACTION**: Veronica should ask ECS's HVAC contractor NOW: "Are you aware of the 179D deduction? We can allocate it to you. How much can you reduce our project cost in exchange?" If the contractor doesn't know about 179D, they're leaving money on the table — and the deduction dies after June.
- **Estimated Value**: Indirect — depends on negotiation. $5.94/sq ft of conditioned space is meaningful for a school building.
