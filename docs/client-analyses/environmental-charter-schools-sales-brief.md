# Environmental Charter Schools — Funding Opportunities Brief

**Client**: Environmental Charter Schools (ECS)
**Prepared for**: Veronica
**Date**: March 18, 2026

---

## About ECS

Environmental Charter Schools is a 4-campus nonprofit public charter school network serving ~1,345 students (grades 6-12) in Lawndale, Gardena, and Inglewood — all designated disadvantaged communities in LA County. They hold the first-ever U.S. DOE Green Ribbon School award. As a 501(c)(3), they're eligible for direct federal cash payments that for-profit entities can't access.

**Active projects**: HVAC replacement (in progress), battery storage + solar (planned), EV charging (interested), landscaping/green infrastructure.

## 1. GRANTS — Direct Funding

### HVAC & Air Quality

**Wildfire Smoke Preparedness in Community Buildings (EPA)**
Competitive grant for HVAC upgrades and air filtration framed as wildfire smoke resilience. ECS has a strong case — their campuses are in census tracts scoring 90-92nd percentile for PM2.5 and up to 98th percentile for childhood asthma. LA County issued smoke advisories affecting their campus areas three times in 18 months.
- **Funding**: Up to $2.5M per award (grant — no repayment)
- **Criteria**: Must demonstrate wildfire smoke exposure risk, serve vulnerable populations, and propose HVAC/filtration improvements. Cost share required.
- **Flag**: Deadline is **April 15, 2026**. Tight timeline — ECS would need to mobilize quickly.

### Facility Financing

**Charter School Facilities Credit Enhancement (CSFA)**
Debt service reserve grants that make bond financing cheaper for charter schools. Very narrow — requires bond financing through CSFA. Related programs (Charter ABLE, Charter FinE) offer bank loan enhancement.
- **Funding**: Up to $1.5M per application. Massively undersubscribed (1 app/year, 100% award rate).
- **Criteria**: Must be pursuing bond or debt financing through CSFA.
- **Flag**: Only relevant if ECS is planning capital financing through bonds. Present as an option, not a lead.

### Landscaping & Water Conservation

**MWD Public Agency Turf Replacement Program**
Rebate for converting grass to drought-tolerant landscaping. Schools qualify as public agencies.
- **Funding**: $3-4/sq ft, up to **$600,000**. Free project assistance included.
- **Criteria**: Must be an MWD member agency (ECS campuses are in West Basin, which is MWD). Prescriptive, first-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: No deadline pressure. Value depends entirely on how much turf ECS has to convert.

**MWD Commercial Turf Replacement Program**
Same concept, commercial tier. Can potentially **stack with the Public Agency program** above.
- **Funding**: $3/sq ft, up to **$150,000**. Closes January 2027.
- **Criteria**: Prescriptive, first-come-first-served. No match required.

**West Basin Public Agency Grass Replacement Rebate**
Redondo Beach, Lawndale, Gardena, and Inglewood are all confirmed West Basin member cities. Includes free landscape design ($2K value).
- **Funding**: $3-5/sq ft, up to **$25,000**. Stacks with MWD programs above.
- **Criteria**: Must be in West Basin service area (confirmed).
- **Flag**: Combined landscaping stack across all three programs: **up to $625K+** if ECS has significant turf to convert.

**West Basin Water Bottle Filling Station Grant**
Small grant for installing water bottle filling stations on campus. Aligns with ECS's environmental mission.
- **Funding**: $1,000 per indoor station, $2,000 per outdoor station. Limit per applicant — but ECS has 4 campuses.
- **Criteria**: Must be in West Basin service area (confirmed). Public facilities eligible.

**SoCal Water$mart Commercial Device Rebates (MWD)**
Per-device rebates for water-efficient fixtures.
- **Funding**: $40-$1,750 per device (toilets, urinals, cooling tower controllers). Small individually but can add up across 4 campuses.
- **Criteria**: Must be MWD member (confirmed). Prescriptive.

## 2. TAX BENEFITS — Cash Back & Deductions

### Battery Storage

**IRS Section 48E Elective Pay — Direct Pay for Battery Storage**
The single most powerful incentive available to ECS. As a tax-exempt school, the IRS sends ECS a **direct cash payment** equal to 30-50% of the battery storage system cost. This is not a credit against taxes — it's a check from the federal government.
- **Funding**: **30% of project cost** returned as cash. Bonus adders can increase to 40-50%:
  - +10% for domestic content compliance
  - +10% for energy community location
  - +10-20% via 48E(h) Low-Income Bonus (see below)
- **Criteria**: Must complete pre-filing registration with the IRS **before** equipment is placed in service. Must meet domestic content requirements for projects over 1 MW (mandatory for direct pay — 100% haircut for non-compliance).
- **Flag**: Battery storage credits are safe through **2033** — no time pressure. Solar credits are effectively closed (BOC deadline July 4, 2026 is too tight for a school that hasn't started planning). **Position this exclusively for battery storage.**
- **Example**: $300K battery system → $90K-$150K cash back from IRS.

**Section 48E(h) Low-Income Communities Bonus Credit**
An additional 10-20% bonus on top of the base 48E credit for projects in disadvantaged communities. Apply separately through DOE.
- **Funding**: **+10% (Category 1)** for facilities in low-income census tracts, or **+20% (Category 4)** if 50% of financial benefits flow to low-income households.
- **Criteria**: Submit application at eco.energy.gov/ejbonus. Project must NOT be placed in service before receiving allocation. ECS has 4 years after allocation to complete the project. DAC status gives priority.
- **Flag**: Application window open now, **closes August 7, 2026**. Initial lottery is over — now first-come-first-served for remaining capacity. **Apply as soon as possible.**

### Solar

**Active Solar Energy System Property Tax Exclusion (CA)**
California excludes the assessed value of solar systems from property tax. Not automatic — must file with the county assessor.
- **Funding**: ~1% of solar system value per year, ongoing. On a $500K system: **~$5K/year in perpetual savings.**
- **Criteria**: File form with LA County Assessor after installation. No application to win.
- **Flag**: **Sunsets January 1, 2027** — any new solar must be in service before then. Also: **ECS already has solar panels on campus.** If they haven't filed this exclusion for their existing solar, they may be overpaying property taxes right now.

## 3. LOANS — Zero-Interest Financing

### HVAC & Energy Efficiency

**K-12 Energy Efficiency Program / KTEP (California Energy Commission)**
Zero-interest revolving loan specifically for K-12 energy efficiency retrofits. Charter schools explicitly eligible.
- **Funding**: Up to **$3M per project**, 0% interest, 15-year repayment. Non-competitive when open.
- **Criteria**: Must be a California LEA. Requires ASHRAE Level 2 energy audit, CEQA/NEPA compliance.
- **Flag**: **PAUSED since January 15, 2026** due to federal IIJA funding uncertainty. CEC issued revised terms in March 2026, suggesting they're preparing to resume. Not dead — monitor and call CEC for reopening timeline. Contact: Marissa Sutton, 916-237-2515.

**SCE On-Bill Financing (OBF)**
Zero-interest loan repaid through the monthly SCE utility bill. No upfront cost, no competition.
- **Funding**: $5K to **$4M**, 0% interest, repaid through utility bill.
- **Criteria**: Must be SCE customer. Loan must be approved before costs incurred. First-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: Backup financing option. Our team has experienced execution/implementation challenges with this program in practice. Keep as a fallback, not a lead.

### Gas Equipment

**SoCalGas Zero Percent On-Bill Financing**
Zero-interest loan for gas equipment upgrades, repaid through the gas bill.
- **Funding**: Up to **$250K** for schools, 15-year terms, 0% interest. No origination fee, no prepayment penalty.
- **Criteria**: 2-year active SoCalGas account in good standing. First-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: Stacks with SoCalGas EERB rebates — use the rebate to reduce principal, finance remainder at 0%.

### Major Capital

**Infrastructure State Revolving Fund / ISRF (CA IBank)**
Below-market AAA-rated infrastructure loans for public facilities including schools.
- **Funding**: $1M to **$65M**. Below-market rates, rolling applications.
- **Criteria**: Educational facilities eligible. No competitive scoring.
- **Flag**: Significant governance decision for a charter school. Only relevant for a major capital project where below-market financing is needed.

## 4. INCENTIVES — Rebates & Performance-Based

### HVAC — Electric

**SCE Express Solutions / Rapid Rebates (via Willdan)**
Prescriptive per-unit rebates for specific HVAC equipment upgrades. Program is narrower than typical — current catalog focuses on VFDs, fan motors, and heat pump water heaters (not standard rooftop unit swaps).
- **Funding**: VFDs: $150-$225/ton | Fan SRM motors: $425-$690/HP | Heat pump water heaters: $6,000-$25,000/unit. $25M remaining for 2026.
- **Criteria**: Must reserve rebate before installation. Prescriptive, first-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: Post-installation docs due **March 31, 2026** — less than 2 weeks. Check whether the HVAC project includes VFDs or fan motor replacements. If yes, submit docs immediately.

**SCE Customized Solutions (via Willdan)**
Performance-based incentives for complex HVAC projects that exceed prescriptive categories. Free technical consultation included.
- **Funding**: Up to **$2.4M per project**, based on verified kWh savings.
- **Criteria**: Requires pre-project baseline engineering analysis. First-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: If the HVAC project is already past the baseline measurement stage, this may not be available. Only for work not covered by Express Solutions.

**SCE Building Improvement / Continuous Energy Improvement (CEI)**
Free dedicated energy expert assigned to ECS for approximately 2 years. Ongoing engagement — facility walk-throughs, energy tracking, ENERGY STAR certification help.
- **Funding**: Free expert engagement + custom performance incentives. Retrocommissioning yields ~15% energy savings.
- **Criteria**: Retrocommissioning requires 25,000+ sq ft with direct digital controls. CEI has no minimum.
- **Flag**: Best applied **after the HVAC project completes** to optimize the new system. Low-effort enrollment — just contact SCE. Also check SCE's **Public Energy Performance (PEP) program** via CLEAResult, which specifically serves schools.

### HVAC — Gas

**SoCalGas Energy Efficiency Rebates for Business (EERB)**
Prescriptive rebates for gas equipment upgrades. Instant rebates available at point of sale through participating distributors.
- **Funding**: Up to **$1M per customer per year**. Boilers: $5-$9 per kBtuh (max $25K/unit). Equipment must be installed within 1 year of purchase.
- **Criteria**: Active SoCalGas account. Prescriptive, first-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: **LA County wildfire-affected customers may get 50% bonus through 12/31/2026.** Check if ECS qualifies. Stacks with SoCalGas OBF.

**SoCalGas Large Public Sector Energy Efficiency Program**
Prescriptive gas rebates plus free no-cost energy audits for public sector facilities.
- **Funding**: Standard prescriptive rebates + custom rebates up to **$0.75/therm saved** for non-listed equipment.
- **Criteria**: **Requires 50,000+ therms annual usage** — must verify ECS's gas consumption. Applications within 60 days of equipment purchase.
- **Flag**: The 60-day window and 50K therm threshold are the key gating questions. Ask ECS about their gas usage.

### EV Charging

**SCE Charge Ready Program (Light-Duty)**
SCE's main EV charging incentive for parking lot chargers — staff and visitor vehicles.
- **Funding**: Minimum **$10K per project**. SCE covers utility-side infrastructure at no cost. DAC status may increase rebate tiers.
- **Criteria**: Must be SCE customer. Equipment purchased after application approval. First-come-first-served.
- **Flag**: No deadline pressure. This is the right program for ECS's stated interest in EV charging for non-fleet vehicles.

**SCE Charge Ready Transport (Heavy-Duty)**
SCE's $356M fleet electrification infrastructure program. Funds charger infrastructure and installation for medium/heavy-duty vehicles (school buses, trucks) — not the vehicles themselves.
- **Funding**: Largest funded EV program in the nation. SCE covers electrical infrastructure.
- **Criteria**: Requires medium/heavy-duty vehicles (Class 3+).
- **Flag**: **Depends on whether ECS operates school buses or other fleet vehicles.** If yes, this is a top-tier opportunity. If no, not applicable. Complementary to the light-duty Charge Ready program — ECS could use both.

### Design & Planning

**California Energy Design Assistance / CEDA (Statewide)**
Free comprehensive energy design assistance and incentives for new construction and major renovations. Administered by PG&E but available statewide across all IOUs including SCE. Implemented by Willdan.
- **Funding**: Free design assistance + performance incentives for qualifying measures.
- **Criteria**: Must be in design phase of new construction or major renovation.
- **Flag**: Not useful for the current HVAC project (already past design). Only relevant if ECS has future new construction or major renovation still in early planning.

### Water & Kitchen (Free Install)

**SoCalGas Energy Efficiency & Rehabilitation Program**
Fully funded direct installation of water heaters and kitchen equipment at zero cost to ECS. SoCalGas sends their own installers.
- **Funding**: **100% funded** — no cost to ECS. Limited to water heaters and kitchen equipment.
- **Criteria**: Contact Eco Entertainment (program administrator) for assessment.
- **Flag**: SoCalGas assigns the installer (no choice). Scope is narrow — no HVAC, no electrical. But free is free. Worth a call if ECS needs cafeteria or kitchen equipment.
